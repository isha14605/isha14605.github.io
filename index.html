Research Student

Laboratory for Metabolic Systems Engineering (Dec. 2021 - May 2022)

Our research project centered around using protein annotations to improve existing genome-scale metabolic models (GSMMs) of bacterial species from the human gut microbiome and establishing a pipeline that can be used to improve GSMMs of other organisms. In this role, I worked on obtaining proteomic data for hundreds of different gut micro-organisms. This involved batch downloading hundreds of GSMMs from the Virtual Metabolic Human (VMH) database and then remotely transferring each file via Tabby, a terminal and SSH Client, to a desktop. In order to speed up the process, I utilized Excel to edit each filename, format it correctly, and convert it to a command in the terminal that would move the file. We wrote a full manuscript for our work, presented a research poster at the UofT Branch Indicium Conference, and published an abstract in the STEM Fellowship Journal.
